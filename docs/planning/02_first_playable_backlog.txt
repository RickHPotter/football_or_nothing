----
Reach a minimal playable football-management loop before expanding the world.

Phase 14 - Loans
----------------
Status: implemented.

Goal:
Support temporary player movement without destroying parent-club contract
history.

Scope:
- Add loan support to the transfer flow.
- Track loan start date, end date, wage contribution, and optional loan fee.
- Keep the parent club contract intact while the player is temporarily
  available for the loan club.
- Include loaned-in players in the loan club squad.
- Exclude loaned-out players from the parent club lineup pool.
- Return players to parent clubs when loans expire.

Implemented:
- `Transfer` and `TransferOffer` support `loan` transfer type and loan end date.
- Loan offers can be submitted and finalized through the transfer market.
- Loan completion pauses the parent contract by setting it non-current without
  terminating it.
- Loan completion creates a current loan contract for the destination club and
  links it to the parent contract.
- Current squad and lineup pools naturally include loaned-in athletes and
  exclude loaned-out athletes through current contract membership.
- `LoanExpiryProcessor` returns expired loan athletes to their parent contracts.
- Season rollover expires loans before normal contract expiry.
- Clubs expose helper methods for loaned-in and loaned-out contracts.

UI:
- Transfer market supports buy and loan offers.
- Open offers and recent transfer lists show transfer type and loan end date.

Deferred:
- Loan recalls.
- Buy options.
- Complex wage splits.
- AI loan negotiations.
- Club dashboard labels for loaned-in and loaned-out players.
- Athlete profile loan context.


Phase 15 - AI Squad Movement
----------------------------
Status: implemented.

Goal:
Make the world move during transfer windows without relying only on the player.

Scope:
- AI clubs inspect squad needs.
- AI clubs sign free agents when affordable.
- AI clubs submit simple transfer offers.
- AI clubs renew or release expired players conservatively.
- Run AI movement during transfer windows and season rollover.

Implemented services:
- `SquadNeedsAnalyzer`
  - Compares current squad positions against a conservative minimum shape.
  - Returns missing positions and counts.
- `AiTransferPlanner`
  - Runs for non-excluded AI clubs during open transfer windows.
  - Targets missing positions first.
  - Prefers affordable free agents.
  - Falls back to affordable permanent transfers.
  - Avoids athletes moved in the recent window and in the current run.
- `AiContractRenewalProcessor`
  - Reviews expiring active non-loan contracts.
  - Renews useful players when the club can absorb the wage.
  - Lets weaker or unaffordable contracts expire through the normal expiry
    processor.
- Season rollover now runs AI renewal, loan expiry, contract expiry, and then
  AI transfer planning before scheduling the next edition.

Rules:
- Clubs target weak positions first.
- Clubs respect transfer and wage budgets.
- Low-reputation or low-budget clubs prefer free agents.
- High-reputation clubs target higher-ability players.
- Avoid moving the same player repeatedly in one window.

UI:
- Transfer market shows recent AI transfers.
- Career dashboard gets a compact world activity panel.

Deferred:
- Bidding wars.
- Player preferences.
- Agents.
- Advanced squad registration.
- Dedicated world activity/news UI.
- AI loans.


Phase 16 - Training
-------------------
Status: implemented.

Goal:
Give the manager a recurring development lever for squad growth and condition.

Scope:
- Add club training focus:
  - balanced
  - fitness
  - attacking
  - defending
  - technical
  - youth development
- Add training intensity.
- Apply training effects on date advancement, month advancement, or season
  rollover.
- Training should affect attributes, condition, morale, and injury risk.

Implemented models:
- `TrainingPlan`
  - club
  - manager
  - focus
  - intensity
  - active_from
- `TrainingResult`
  - training plan
  - club
  - athlete
  - changed attribute
  - old value
  - new value
  - condition change
  - occurred_on

Implemented behavior:
- Club dashboard exposes training focus and intensity controls.
- Date advancement applies the active training plan to the current squad.
- Training focus chooses the attribute group being developed.
- Intensity changes condition pressure and growth speed.
- Young/high-potential players benefit from development-oriented training.
- Training results are stored and shown on the club dashboard.

Rules:
- High intensity improves faster but lowers condition.
- Low condition should increase injury risk in later match simulation phases.
- Young high-potential athletes improve more.
- Older athletes maintain or decline.

UI:
- Club dashboard training panel.
- Club dashboard recent training result history.

Deferred:
- Staff modifiers.
- Facilities.
- Individual player training.
- Tactical familiarity.
- Athlete profile development history.
- Career dashboard monthly training summary.


Phase 17 - Scouting
-------------------
Status: implemented.

Goal:
Make player discovery and information uncertainty part of recruitment.

Scope:
- Add scouting assignments.
- Add scouting reports.
- Hide or fuzz information for unscouted players.
- Reveal ability, potential, wage expectations, and fit over time.
- Support basic searches by position, country, reputation, and ability range.

Likely models:
- `ScoutReport`
  - athlete
  - club
  - observed current ability
  - observed potential ability
  - confidence
  - summary
  - created_on
- `ScoutingAssignment`
  - club
  - country or position
  - focus
  - status
  - starts_on
  - ends_on

Implemented behavior:
- Managers can create club scouting assignments from a scouting screen.
- Assignments can target country, position, and focus.
- Date advancement processes assignments whose end date has passed.
- Completed assignments generate scout reports for matching players outside the
  manager's current squad.
- Reports store observed ability, observed potential, confidence, summary, and
  report date.
- Transfer market displays observed ability and confidence for scouted players
  and hides exact ability for unscouted players.

UI:
- New scouting screen.
- Transfer market shows known vs unscouted players.

Deferred:
- Staff scout quality.
- Hidden attributes.
- International restrictions.
- Scouting costs.
- Athlete profile report confidence for non-owned targets.
- Advanced searches by reputation and ability range.

Phase 19 - Youth Academy
------------------------
Status: implemented.

Goal:
Create a long-term squad-building path through generated youth prospects.

Scope:
- Add yearly youth intake.
- Generate youth athletes for clubs.
- Youth potential depends on club reputation, country reputation, and academy
  quality.
- Allow promotion to senior squad.

Implemented models:
- Club field `academy_quality`.
- `YouthIntake`
  - club
  - season_year
  - generated_on
- Athlete fields:
  - youth_intake
  - youth_academy_player
  - academy_graduate

Implemented behavior:
- Clubs can generate one youth intake per season.
- Intake prospects are generated as academy athletes without senior contracts.
- Prospect potential depends on club reputation, country reputation, and academy
  quality.
- Managers can promote academy prospects into the senior squad.
- Promotion creates a current athlete contract and marks the athlete as an
  academy graduate.

Rules:
- Bigger clubs produce better youth on average.
- Low-reputation clubs can still rarely produce excellent prospects.
- Youth players start with lower current ability and higher growth runway.

UI:
- Club dashboard youth academy panel.
- Youth intake screen.
- Athlete profile academy graduate marker.

Deferred:
- Youth competitions.
- Youth coaches.
- Training groups.
- Youth poaching.
- Academy investment and operating costs.
- Richer intake personalities and rare outliers.
